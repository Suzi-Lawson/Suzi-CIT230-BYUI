Tokyo Japan Temple

Announced on August 9th, 1975 and dedicated on October 27th, 1980 by President Spencer W. Kimball, the Tokyo Japan Temple (formally known as Tokyo Temple) became the 20th temple built by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints. It is also the first temple to be built in Asia. Until the announcement on April 10th, 2017 and the actual closure of the temple in October 2017 for renovations, Tokyo Temple was the 18th temple of the church in opperation. Completion and rededication is anticipated in the year 2020. The Tokyo Japan Temple is a beautiful structure that sits on less than half an acre of land. It is bordered on one side by a Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints stake center and sits across from Arisugawa-no-miya Memorial Park. The temple was originally built without an Angel of Moroni statue. A ceromony on December 10, 2004 took place to add the iconic symbol of the church.

About Us

The Temple Inn & Suites is a hotel chain headquartered in Bethesda, MD that builds and manages specialized hotel accommodations near temple locations of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints around the world. The owner-operated company caters to temple workers, patrons, and their families by providing specialized services and accommodations that meet the needs of those patrons. The hotel accommodations and services are available to anyone willing to abide by the terms and policies outlined by the company.
